Explain what the form element is for and what two attributes it should always include.
Answer: A form is for collect user's data, and the two attributes it should always include are action and method.

Explain what form controls are at a high level.
Answer: There are elements users will interact with on the form.

What is the name attribute for?
Answer: The name attribute serves as a reference to the data inputted into a form control after submitting it.

What are the three most common form controls you can use for allowing users to select predefined options?
Answer: Checkboxes, radio buttons and dropdowns.

What are the three types of buttons in HTML?
Answer: submit, button and reset.

What are the two most challenging aspects of styling forms?
Answer: Default browser styles and tricky and downright impossible to style some form controls.
